Study Techniques Explained

1. Pomodoro Technique: Study in focused intervals of 25-30 minutes followed by a 5-minute break. This helps maintain concentration and reduces fatigue while making study sessions more productive.

2. Active Recall: Test yourself frequently instead of just rereading. Asking questions and recalling answers strengthens memory and understanding of the material.

3. Mind Mapping: Visualize your notes in diagrams connecting ideas and concepts. This technique is useful for subjects like Science and History to see relationships clearly.

4. Flashcards: Use cards to memorize facts, vocabulary, formulas, or definitions. Repetition and self-testing with flashcards help retain information effectively.

5. Teaching Others: Explain what you have learned to a friend, classmate, or even to yourself aloud. Teaching forces you to organize your thoughts and ensures you understand the topic deeply.

6. Sticky Notes / Visual Cues: Use sticky notes or visual reminders around your study space to reinforce key concepts. They help with memorization and keep important points visible.
